Prickly Pear Cactus Salsa

Active time: 15 minutes

Total time: 15 minutes

Serves

Cactus Salsa, also known as Mexican Nopales, is a Mexican classic and is fantastic with tortilla chips!

Ingredients

  • 1 pound cactus (nopales, prickly pear cactus paddles)
  • 3 tomatoes, roma
  • 1 onion, red
  • 1 cup white corn, frozen
  • 2 jalape√±os, deveined and chopped
  • 1 cup cilantro, roughly chopped
  • 1 lime
  • 1 tablespoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon sea salt
  • 1 clove garlic, chopped

Directions

  1. Clean and dice the cactus. Add the raw nopales and cover with water. Cook with a tablespoon of sea salt for 10-15 minutes covered. When ready remove the water and rinse several times with iced or cold until slime disappears.
  2. Roast the corn kernels: Heat a cast iron or stick-free skillet with 1 teaspoon olive oil over medium heat on the stove top. When the pan is heated, add frozen corn and spread out in an even layer. Do not stir the corn. Allow it to roast in the hot pan for about 2 to 3 minutes.
  3. Chop all remaining vegetables and mix everything together with the cooked cactus. Season with sea salt, lime juice, and cumin.
  4. Use the cactus salsa as an appetizer with corn chips or as a garnish for salads, tacos, sopes, or tostadas.
  5. For a smoother salsa, blend desired proportion of the salsa in a food processor. For chunky, blend about 50% of the salsa.
