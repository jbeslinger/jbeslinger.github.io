Cheddar Cheese Sauce

Active time: 10 minutes

Total time: 10 minutes

Serves

Try it with steamed or sautéed veggies, baked potatoes, nachos, open-faced roast beef sandwiches, and hot cooked macaroni or penne. Using sharp cheddar cheese is important here, as you want the zippy flavor mild just doesn't have.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup low-fat milk
  • 4 teaspoons all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 1/3 cup sharp cheddar cheese, shredded
  • 1/4 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper

Directions

Tip: Remove from heat, then stir in the cheese to ensure that the sauce doesn't curdle. The flour helps stabilize the dairy mixture, but if it's boiled after the cheese is added, the sauce will separate or curdle.

  1. Combine 1/4 of the milk and flour in a saucepan; stir with a whisk. Stir in remaining 3/4 of the milk and salt; bring to a boil over medium heat, stirring frequently.
  2. Reduce heat to low; simmer 2 minutes or until slightly thickened, stirring constantly.
  3. Remove from heat. Stir in cheese and pepper, stirring until cheese melts.
