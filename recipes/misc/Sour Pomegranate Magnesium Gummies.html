Sour Pomegranate Magnesium Gummies

Active time: 10 minutes

Total time: 3 hours

Servings: gummies

Sour, sweet, oddly shaped, and heaping with vitamins. These are a great way to get rid of that magnesium citrate that you purchased for $16 and can't drink with water (like how the damn label wants you to).

Ingredients

  • 3/4 cup unsweetened pomegranate juice (or whatever juice you want)
  • 3 tablespoons gelatin
  • 2 tablespoons magnesium citrate powder

    • Also required

  • Small silicone candy mold of your choice

Directions

  1. In a small saucepan, add juice, sea salt, and magnesium. Sprinkle the gelatin powder over the juice mixture in the saucepan (no heat yet) and whisk to combine. Allow this mixture to sit for about 5 minutes. This is called "blooming" the gelatin.
  2. Turn burner to low-medium heat. As the mixture heats up, it will start to liquify. Once the gelatin is dissolved, turn off the heat. Whisk once more to combine.
  3. Pour the mixture into molds. You can also pour the mixture into a baking dish and cut out the shapes later if you have no mold. Also, be sure to use cooking spray to make sure they pop out easily.
  4. Refrigerate the molds for a few hours or until fully set. Then carefully pop out the molds and place in a storage container in the fridge.
