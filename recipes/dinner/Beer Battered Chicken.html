Beer Battered Chicken

Active time: 30 minutes

Total time: 30 minutes

Serves

Easy to make, beer battered, pan fried chicken. Guaranteed to make your bowels & Long John Silver, himself, cry.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 pounds boneless chicken breasts, halved
  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 1/2 cup cold beer (the hoppier, the better)
  • 3 cups vegetable oil, for frying
  • 2 cups stuff

Directions

  1. Rinse chicken and slice into 1 inch strips. In a medium bowl, mix together flour, salt, pepper, and baking powder. In another bowl, mix together the eggs and beer.
  2. Coat chicken strips in egg wash, then toss them in the flour. Repeat once more for each strip, only doing a few chicken strips at a time. Lay the strips on a wire rack to dry for about 10 minutes.
  3. While drying, heal the oil in a deep, heavy, pan to 375°.
  4. Frying a few at a time to ensure equal cooking, gently lay each strip in the hot oil and fry on both sides until golden brown and internal measures 160°.
  5. Remove and place on wire rack to cool.
