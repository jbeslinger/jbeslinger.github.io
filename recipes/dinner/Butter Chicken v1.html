Indian Butter Chicken

Active time: 20 minutes

Total time: 30 minutes

Serves

Butter chicken is a heavenly dish from the gods. It's also pretty good for you. Serve with rice and naan/roti.

Ingredients

    For the sauce

  • 6 tablespoons butter, divided (or ghee/olive oil)
  • 2 pounds boneless skinless chicken thighs, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 cup low-fat yogurt (plain Greek)
  • 1 onion, diced yellow
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, grated
  • 2 tomatoes, roma
  • 1/2 can tomato paste
  • 1 tablespoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon brown sugar

    • For the rice

  • 2 tablespoons butter (or ghee/olive oil)
  • 1 cup basmati rice

    • For garnishing

  • Naan bread
  • Lime juice
  • Cilantro

Directions

    For the rice

  1. Place the rice into a bowl, and cover with cold tap water. Mix with your fingers until the water is cloudy. Strain and repeat several more times.
  2. Place the rice into the rice cooker with butter and bottled water. The ratio of water to rice is 2:1.
  3. When the rice cooker paddle lifts up, indicating done-ness, let the rice cooker sit, unplugged, and steaming for 10 more minutes.

    4. For the sauce

  4. Melt a third of the butter in a large skillet over medium-high heat, brown the pieces of the chicken so each side is browned & cooked fully. Set aside when you’re done.
  5. Melt another third of the butter in the pan over medium heat. Add the onion, and cook until beginning to soften — about three minutes. Combine the dry spices in a bowl while the onion is cooking.
  6. While the onion is cooking, blanche & skin the tomatoes. Let the tomatoes sit in the ice bath for a minute to cool down.
  7. Add the garlic & ginger to the pan for carmelization (about 2 minutes).
  8. Add the dry spices, tomato paste, and hand crush the tomatoes. Bring the mixture to a simmer and let cook for five minutes.
  9. Optionally, you can blend this sauce if you prefer smooth.
  10. Put the yogurt into a separate bowl. Stirring continuously, add 1/4 cup at a time of the tomato sauce to the yogurt until you've double the volume of the yogurt (tempering the yogurt to prevent curdling).
  11. Bring the mixture back to a simmer, add the browned chicken, and let simmer for 10-15 minutes. Keep the heat low here — not a rolling boil.
  12. Stir in the remaining third of butter, and season with salt and pepper, to taste.
  13. Serve garnished with lime and cilantro, alongside rice and naan.
