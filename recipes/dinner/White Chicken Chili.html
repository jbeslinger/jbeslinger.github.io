White Chicken Chili

Active time: minutes

Total time: minutes

Serves

Warming, creamy, perfectly comforting. Great alternative to beef chili, easier on the stomach, and is a simple, delicious dinner.

Ingredients

    For chili

  • 1 onion, yellow small, diced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 29 ounces low-sodium chicken broth
  • 7 ounces green chilis, canned
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons ground cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 8 ounces Neufchatel cheese (light cream cheese), cut into small cubes
  • 1 1/4 cups frozen corn
  • 30 ounces cannellini beans, canned
  • 2 1/2 cups shredded cooked rotisserie chicken

    • For garnish

  • Lime juice
  • Chopped, fresh cilantro
  • Tortilla chips or strips, monterrey jack cheese, sliced avocado

Directions

  1. Heat olive oil in a dutch oven over medium-heat. Add onion and saute for 4 minutes or until translucent, then add garlic and saute further for a minute.
  2. Add chicken broth, green chilies, cumin, paprika, oregano, coriander, cayenne pepper, and season with salt and pepper. Bring mixture to a boil and reduce heat to simmer for 15 minutes.
  3. Drain and rinse beans then measure out a cup. Set whole beans aside and transfer 1 cup of beans to a food processor with 1/4 cup chicken broth. Puree until nearly smooth.
  4. Add cheese to soup along with corn, whole beans, and pureed beans. Stir will and simmer for 5-10 minutes longer.
  5. Stir in chicken, fresh lime juice, and cilantro. Serve with monterrey jack cheese, cilantro, avocado slices, and tortilla chips if desired.
