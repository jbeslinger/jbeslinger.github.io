Ratatouille

Active time: 20 minutes

Total time: 80 minutes

Serves

Calories:

Per serving:

Ingredients

    Vegetables

  • 2 eggplants, japanese
  • 6 tomatos, roma
  • 2 squashes, yellow
  • 2 zucchinis

    • Sauce

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, diced, yellow
  • 4 cloves garlic
  • 1 pepper, red bell, diced
  • 1 pepper, yellow bell, diced
  • 28 ounces crushed tomatoes
  • 2 tablespoons basil leaves, chopped

    • Herb seasoning

  • 2 tablespoons basil leaves, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons parsley leaves, chopped
  • 2 teaspoons fresh thyme
  • 4 tablespoons olive oil

Directions

  1. Preheat oven for 375.
  2. Slice the eggplant, tomatoes, squash, and zucchinis into approximately 1mm rounds. (A mandolin is perfect for this.) Set aside.
  3. Heat the olive oil in a 12-inch, oven-safe pan over medium-high heat. Saut√© the onion, garlic, and bell peppers until soft, about 10 minutes. Season with salt and pepper, then add the crushed tomatoes. Stir ingredients until fully incorporated. Remove from heat and blend for smoothness (optional).
  4. Back in the pan, add basil leaves and stir once more. Then smooth the surface with a spatula.
  5. Arrange the sliced veggies in alternating patterns. (i.e. eggplant, tomato, squash, zucchini.) Arrange these on top of the sauce from the outer edge to the middle of the pan. Season with salt and pepper.
  6. Cover with foil and bake for 40 minutes. Uncover and bake for 20 more minutes.
  7. Mix herb seasoning together while this is cooking. Pour the herbs over the cooked ratatouille.
  8. Serve hot or reheat the next day in an foil-covered, oven-save recepticle at 350 for 15 minutes.
