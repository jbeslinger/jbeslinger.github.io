Palak Paneer

Active time: 25 minutes

Total time: 35 minutes

Serves

One of the great Indian curry dishes. Full of dark green spinach, creamy, and delicious. Only problems: it's a little time-consuming, paneer is impossible to find, & I modified the recipe before I made it again. Best of luck.

Ingredients

    For the sauce

  • 2 bunches spinach, roughly chopped
  • 1 bunch fenugreek leaves (kasoori methi), roughly chopped
  • 2 tablespoons canola oil (or olive oil/ghee)
  • 1 onion, red, chopped
  • 14 1/2 ounces tomato sauce
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon garam masala
  • 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, grated
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon cayenne powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 cup low-fat yogurt (plain Greek)

    • For the paneer

  • 1/2 pound paneer, cubed
  • 1 tablespoon canola oil (or olive oil/ghee)

Directions

    For the sauce

  1. Bring a pot of water to boil. Add spinach & fenugreek to water until wilted. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a blender, process half the leaves until coarse pureed. Rough chop the remaining half and set aside.
  3. In a heavy-bottom skillet, heat oil and add paneer cubes. Fry until light brown and crispy on most sides. Remove on paper towel and set aside.
  4. In the same pan, add more oil and fry the onions/garlic on medium heat until translucent (10 minutes).
  5. While this is cooking, mix together the dry spices in a separate bowl.
  6. Combine the onions/garlic with the dry spices and tomato sauce. Let simmer for 10 more minutes, covered.
  7. Add in the coarse pureed spinach, the chopped spinach and paneer and let cook for 5-10 minutes covered.
  8. Put the yogurt into a separate bowl. Stirring continuously, add 1/4 cup at a time of the spinach sauce to the yogurt until you've double the volume of the yogurt (tempering the yogurt to prevent curdling).
  9. Put it all back into the skillet & let simmer for 10-15 minutes. Keep the heat low here â€” not a rolling boil. The spinach will darken here.
  10. Salt and pepper to taste.

    11. For the rice

  11. Place the rice into a bowl, and cover with cold tap water. Mix with your fingers until the water is cloudy. Strain and repeat several more times.
  12. Place the rice into the rice cooker with butter and bottled water. The ratio of water to rice is 2:1.
  13. When the rice cooker paddle lifts up, indicating done-ness, let the rice cooker sit, unplugged, and steaming for 10 more minutes.
