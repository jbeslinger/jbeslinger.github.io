Palak Paneer

Active time: 25 minutes

Total time: 35 minutes

Serves

Calories:

Per serving:

One of the great Indian curry dishes. Full of dark green spinach, creamy, and delicious. Only problems: it's a little time-consuming, paneer is impossible to find, & I modified the recipe before I made it again. Best of luck.

Ingredients

    For the sauce

  • 1 bunch of spinach leaves
  • 3 tbsp ghee
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tbsp garlic, finely chopped
  • 4-5 chillies, Dry Red
  • 2 onions, finely chopped
  • 1 tomato, finely chopped
  • 1 tsp coriander seed powder
  • 1 tsp garam Masala powder
  • 1/2 kg paneer (chicken is also a great sub w/ less fat)
  • Salt
  • Fresh Cream

Directions

    For the sauce

  1. Boil water in a vessel, add salt, spinach leaves, keep them in the boiling water for 3-4 minutes and then transfer it on an cold water.
  2. Let the spinach cool down and then grind it into a nice puree.
  3. Heat a wok, add ghee, cumin seeds, garlic, red chillies, onions and saute it until it turns soft.
  4. Add tomato, cook it till they are soft.
  5. Add coriander powder, garam masala, mix it well and add the spinach puree.
  6. Add salt and give it a good mix.
  7. Add paneer, cook it on medium flame for 4-5 minutes.
  8. Take it out in a bowl and garnish it with fresh cream.
  9. Our Palak Paneer is ready to serve.

    10. For the rice

  10. Place the rice into a bowl, and cover with cold tap water. Mix with your fingers until the water is cloudy. Strain and repeat several more times.
  11. Place the rice into the rice cooker with butter and bottled water. The ratio of water to rice is 2:1.
  12. When the rice cooker paddle lifts up, indicating done-ness, let the rice cooker sit, unplugged, and steaming for 10 more minutes.
