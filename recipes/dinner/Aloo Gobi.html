Aloo Gobi

Active time: 30 minutes

Total time: 30 minutes

Serves

Aloo Gobi is a very classic Indian dish of potatoes and cauliflower. It's a one pot, super simple, comforting dish that's ready in 30 minutes flat.

Ingredients

  • 3 tablespoons cooking oil, (or any sub)
  • 2 potatoes, medium russet, cubed (1-1.5")
  • 1 head cauliflower, florets
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 onion, small red, diced
  • 2 tomatoes, medium, chopped
  • 1 teaspoons cumin seeds
  • 1/4 teaspoon ginger powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon kasoori methi (fenugreek) leaves, dried
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala

Directions

  1. Heat 2/3 teaspoon of oil in a pan on medium heat. Add cauliflower florets and fry for 2-3 minutes and then add the sliced potatoes. Fry on medium-low flame for 7-8 minutes till potatoes and cauliflower have some brown spots on them. Drain on a tissue paper and set aside.
  2. In the same pan heat 1/3 teaspoon of oil on medium heat and add cumin seeds and let them crackle.
  3. Add the onions and cook for 2 minutes till translucent.
  4. Then add the garlic and ginger powder on top of that and cook for 2 extra minutes.
  5. Then add the chopped tomatoes and cook for 2 minutes until they are little soft.
  6. Add turmeric, red chili, coriander, & kasoori methi. Cover the pan and let the masala cook for 2-3 minutes, then add potatoes.
  7. Add garam masala and cook the potato & cauliflower on medium-low heat for 5-6 minutes. Stir occasionally.
  8. Add salt to taste and cover the pan to cook for additional 6-7 minutes on low flame or until potato and cauliflower are tender, but not soggy. If the masala sticks, add a tablespoon of water in at a time.
  9. Garnish with cilantro leaves and serve with any Indian bread.
